About

Why this blog exists, who it's for, and what you'll find here.

Our mission

AI in Plain English exists for one reason: to make artificial intelligence approachable for everyone who isn't an engineer. No jargon. No gatekeeping. No assuming you already know what "LLM," "prompt engineering," or "multimodal" mean.

If you've ever heard a friend rave about ChatGPT and thought, "that sounds useful, but where do I even start?" — this site is built for you.

What you'll find here

  • Tool guides — plain-English overviews of ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and the other major AI tools, including how to sign up, what they cost, and what they're best at.
  • New-release explainers — when a big model drops (like GPT Image 2 or Claude Opus 4.7), we write it up in everyday language within 24 hours.
  • Real case studies — not cherry-picked demos. Actual jobs we tried with real tools: a café menu, a product shoot, a children's book, a photo retouch.
  • How-tos — step-by-step walkthroughs for common tasks like writing better prompts, making images, summarizing documents, or generating video.
  • A built-in chatbot — every page has an "Ask" button in the corner. Type any AI question and it'll answer using information from our posts.

Who we are

We're a small, independent team of writers and tinkerers who use these tools every day. We're not sponsored by any AI company, and we don't take affiliate commissions on the tools we recommend. If we say something's great, it's because we've used it.

Our principles

If your grandmother couldn't follow it, it's not plain English yet.
  • No jargon without a definition. Every technical term gets explained the first time it appears.
  • Honest about limits. Every tool has flaws. We call them out, especially when they affect real-world use (hallucinations, privacy, cost, ethics).
  • Fast, not flashy. The site is lightweight on purpose. No tracking cookies, no pop-ups, no autoplay anything.
  • Updated constantly. AI moves fast. If a tool changes significantly, we update the post — not bury it.

Get in touch

Have a question we haven't answered? A tool you want us to review? A mistake you spotted? Tap the chat button in the bottom-right on any page, or reach out directly — we read every message.
