The Best AI Tools for Making Pictures (and When to Use Each One)

You've probably seen jaw-dropping AI-generated images floating around online — dreamy portraits, wild landscapes, product shots that never existed. All of that comes from image generation AI: tools where you type a description and the computer draws the picture for you.

There are a handful of good ones, and they're each a little different. Here's the plain-English tour.

The Main Image Generators

  • Midjourney — the one famous for gorgeous, painterly, almost cinematic images. Strong at artistic styles and moody lighting. Works through a web app (and historically through Discord).
  • DALL·E 3 — made by OpenAI and built into ChatGPT. Best if you want to generate images inside a normal chat, edit them conversationally, and you already pay for ChatGPT Plus.
  • Adobe Firefly — Adobe's image generator, baked into Photoshop and Express. The big selling point: it's trained on licensed content, so the images are safer to use commercially.
  • Stable Diffusion — the open-source option. It runs on your own computer (with the right graphics card) and gives you the most control. Steeper learning curve.
  • Google Imagen — Google's image model, available inside Gemini and Google Workspace. Fast and well-integrated if you already live in Google's tools.

Which One Should I Use?

🎨
Want beautiful, artistic images? Start with Midjourney.
💬
Want to just ask in chat? Use DALL·E inside ChatGPT.
💼
Need commercial-safe images? Adobe Firefly.

What About Editing Existing Photos?

If you want to modify a photo instead of creating one from scratch — remove a background, extend an image, change a color, swap an outfit — these are great:

  • Adobe Photoshop's Generative Fill — the easiest if you already use Photoshop. Select an area, type what you want, done.
  • Canva Magic Edit — similar idea, but inside Canva. Works well for social media posts and quick marketing graphics.
  • ChatGPT (with DALL·E) — you can upload a picture and ask it to change specific things in conversation.

A note on "prompts"

The description you type is called a prompt. Better prompts make better images. Be specific: say the subject, the style ("watercolor," "cinematic photo," "3D render"), the mood, and the lighting. "A cat" is boring. "A curious orange tabby cat peeking out from behind a stack of vintage books, warm afternoon light, shallow depth of field" gets you somewhere special.

What Do They Cost?

Most image AI tools use a credit or subscription model:

  • Midjourney: starts at $10/month for a basic plan.
  • DALL·E 3: included with ChatGPT Plus ($20/month).
  • Adobe Firefly: free tier exists; full access comes with an Adobe Creative Cloud subscription.
  • Stable Diffusion: free if you run it yourself; paid hosted versions exist.
  • Google Imagen: included with Gemini Advanced ($20/month).

Quick Start

If you've never tried image AI before, here's the 5-minute on-ramp: open ChatGPT (if you have Plus) or Gemini, and ask it to draw something simple — a logo for your coffee shop, a poster for your band, a portrait of your dog as a wizard. See what comes out. Tweak the wording. Try again. That back-and-forth is the skill.
