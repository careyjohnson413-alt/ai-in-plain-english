AI Tools That Make Videos: A Beginner's Guide

A couple of years ago, "AI-generated video" meant a blurry, melting animation that lasted three seconds. Today, you can type a sentence and get a cinematic clip that looks like it came out of a real camera. The tools moved fast — here's what's actually usable right now.

The Main Video Generators

  • Sora — OpenAI's video model. Generates up to a minute of realistic, high-resolution video from a text prompt. Available through ChatGPT Plus and Pro plans.
  • Runway — the pioneer. Runway's Gen-3 model is excellent for short cinematic clips, and their web app includes tons of extra editing tools (green screen, slow motion, style transfer).
  • Google Veo — Google's text-to-video model, available inside Gemini and for creators in Google's Vids and YouTube Shorts tools. Strong at realistic motion and camera moves.
  • Pika — friendly and fast, great for fun social clips, animations, and adding motion to still images.
  • Luma Dream Machine — known for smooth, dreamlike motion and believable character animation.

Which Tool for Which Job?

🎬
Cinematic storytelling clips? Sora or Runway.
📱
Fast social media videos? Pika or Veo.
🖼️
Animate a still photo? Pika or Luma.

Talking-Head and Avatar Videos

A different kind of video AI makes a virtual person say whatever you type. Great for explainer videos, training content, or product demos where you don't want to film yourself.

  • HeyGen — realistic AI avatars in dozens of languages. You can even clone your own voice and face.
  • Synthesia — the business favorite. Used heavily for corporate training and internal comms.
  • D-ID — turns a still photo into a talking head that speaks your script.

A word of warning

AI video of real people (friends, celebrities, politicians) saying things they didn't say is a deepfake — and it's both ethically and legally risky. Most reputable AI video tools require consent for voice and face cloning. Don't skip that.

Editing Videos with AI

You don't have to generate a video from scratch to use AI. Some of the most useful tools enhance footage you already have:

  • Descript — edit video by editing the transcript, like editing a Word document. Amazing for podcasts and interviews.
  • CapCut — free mobile/desktop editor with AI-powered auto-captions, background removal, and voice cloning.
  • Adobe Premiere's AI tools — auto-captioning, filler-word removal, and scene detection, right inside the industry-standard editor.

What Do They Cost?

  • Sora: included with ChatGPT Plus ($20/month) with limited generations; Pro plans get more.
  • Runway: free tier for a few seconds; paid plans start around $15/month.
  • Pika and Luma: free tiers available; paid plans $10–$30/month.
  • HeyGen and Synthesia: start around $24/month.

What to Expect

AI video is wild but not yet perfect. Expect weirdness with hands, fast motion, and long-held subjects. Keep prompts short and specific, lean into stylized looks (animation, dreamy, cinematic) where flaws are easier to hide, and plan for several takes. This technology is improving month-over-month — the clip that looks "almost right" today will look seamless by next year.
